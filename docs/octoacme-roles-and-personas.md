# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure the user experience is seamless, accessible, and aligned with business goals. They translate user needs into intuitive workflows and interfaces throughout the product lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Design product workflows, wireframes, and interfaces
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Collaborate with Product Managers and Developers for iterative design improvements
- Define and maintain design standards and accessibility guidelines
- Validate designs against acceptance criteria before development begins

### Goals
- Deliver experiences that are intuitive, accessible, and delightful
- Reduce usability issues discovered late in the cycle
- Build a shared design language across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and Developers before sprints begin
- Usability test readouts shared with Product and Project leads
- Handoff notes and annotated mockups in the project repository

### Collaboration & Hand-offs
- **With Product Managers:** Align on user needs, problem statements, and success metrics before design begins.
- **With Developers:** Provide annotated mockups and clarify design intent during implementation; review builds for fidelity.
- **With Project Managers:** Flag design dependencies or open questions that could affect timelines.
- **With Business Analysts:** Incorporate business process flows and stakeholder requirements into UX decisions.

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers streamline infrastructure, deployment, and operations to support continuous delivery and system reliability. They bridge development and operations to enable fast, safe releases.

### Responsibilities
- Automate deployments and manage CI/CD pipelines
- Monitor system health and respond to infrastructure incidents
- Ensure infrastructure scalability, security compliance, and cost efficiency
- Maintain environment configurations and infrastructure-as-code
- Partner with Developers on build tooling and local environment setup

### Goals
- Enable reliable, repeatable deployments with minimal manual intervention
- Reduce mean time to recovery (MTTR) for incidents
- Maintain secure, compliant, and observable infrastructure

### Typical Communication
- Release planning sessions with Project Managers and Developers
- On-call handoff notes and incident post-mortems
- Infrastructure change announcements and runbook updates

### Collaboration & Hand-offs
- **With Developers:** Define CI/CD pipeline requirements, support local dev environment setup, and review infrastructure impact of code changes.
- **With Project Managers:** Provide release readiness status and flag infrastructure risks or deployment blockers.
- **With Product Managers:** Communicate planned downtime, rollout strategies, and environment constraints.
- **With Customer Support Lead:** Share incident updates and timelines to enable accurate user communications.

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts translate business requirements into actionable items for the technical team. They act as a bridge between stakeholders and the delivery team to ensure solutions address the right problems.

### Responsibilities
- Gather, document, and validate stakeholder requirements
- Map business processes and identify technical constraints
- Support Product Managers in backlog refinement and acceptance criteria definition
- Facilitate requirements workshops and sign-off sessions
- Identify and surface scope changes or conflicting requirements early

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are complete, unambiguous, and testable before work begins
- Reduce rework caused by misunderstood or missing requirements
- Maintain a clear audit trail of business decisions

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- User stories and process flow documentation shared with PM and engineering
- Change request summaries distributed to relevant stakeholders

### Collaboration & Hand-offs
- **With Product Managers:** Co-author user stories and acceptance criteria; flag conflicting or incomplete requirements.
- **With Project Managers:** Surface scope changes and dependencies that affect the project plan.
- **With Developers:** Clarify business intent behind requirements and validate that implementations meet business needs.
- **With UX Designers:** Share business process flows and stakeholder constraints to inform UX decisions.

---

## Customer Support Lead

### Role Summary
The Customer Support Lead serves as the voice of the customer post-release and manages support workflows. They surface user feedback and issues to the product and engineering teams to drive continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Aggregate user feedback, bug reports, and support tickets
- Surface recurring issues and trends to Product and Developer teams
- Own incident communications to users during outages or service disruptions
- Maintain support knowledge base and escalation procedures
- Participate in retrospectives to share customer impact data

### Goals
- Minimize time-to-resolution for customer-reported issues
- Ensure customers receive accurate, timely communications during incidents
- Close the feedback loop between users and the product team

### Typical Communication
- Weekly support digest shared with Product and Project Managers
- Incident updates coordinated with DevOps and Project Manager
- Retrospective summaries highlighting top customer pain points

### Collaboration & Hand-offs
- **With Product Managers:** Share support trends and customer feedback to inform prioritization and roadmap decisions.
- **With Project Managers:** Coordinate communication plans for planned maintenance or release-related disruptions.
- **With DevOps Engineers:** Receive incident updates to relay accurate status information to customers.
- **With Developers:** Escalate reproducible bugs with supporting evidence (logs, screenshots, reproduction steps).
